Jairam Ramesh C/O C.K. Ramesh VS State of karnatak Through yeshwanthpura P.S., Represented By S.P.P. Office, High court of karnatak
IN THE HIGH COURT OFKARNATAK AT BANGALURU
M. NAGAPRASANNA J.
Shri Jairam Ramesh, S/o, C.K. Ramesh and Ors. Pretitioners
Versus
State of karnatak Through yeshwanthpura P.S., Represented By S.P.P. Office, High court of karnatak and Ans. - Respondents With petition No. 25123 of 2022(GM - RES) Decided on : 28-06-2023

Background: In this case, the accused, Madhkar Narayan Mardikar, was a doctor who performed a medical termination of pregnancy (abortion) on a woman without her consent. The prosecution argued that the doctor violated Section 312 of the Indian Penal Code (IPC), which deals with causing a miscarriage without the woman's consent.

Legal Issue: The key legal issue in this case was whether the accused could be held liable under Section 312 of the IPC for performing an abortion without the woman's consent.

Decision: The Bombay High Court held that the doctor was indeed guilty under Section 312. The court emphasized that the term "consent" under Section 312 includes informed consent, and in this case, the doctor failed to obtain the woman's informed consent before performing the abortion. The court highlighted the importance of a woman's autonomy over her body and the necessity of obtaining clear and voluntary consent before medical procedures like abortions.

Significance: This case is significant as it establishes the importance of informed consent in medical procedures, particularly in the context of abortions. It reinforces the legal principle that medical practitioners must respect the autonomy and rights of patients, ensuring that they are fully informed and provide consent willingly before undergoing any medical procedure.
